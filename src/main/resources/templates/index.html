Biography:

Wolfgang Gabriel (* June 9, 1930 in Vienna) is an Austrian composer, conductor and music teacher. The son of a psychiatrist and a teacher, played the piano already as a child. After WWII he attended the Realgymnasium until 1948. From 1946 he also took piano lessons with Hans Sittner, later president of the Academy for Music and Performing Arts in Vienna, and music theory lessons with Leo Lehner, the founder of the choir "Jung-Wien". In 1947 he entered the Academy of Music and Performing Arts in Vienna. There he studied piano with Grete Hinterhofer, music theory with Joseph Marx (later with Alfred Uhl) and conducting in the Kapellmeister class of Hans Swarowsky from 1950. In 1952 he completed his studies with distinction and a graduation prize. From 1954 to 1996 Gabriel taught at the Vienna Academy of Music, first as a répétiteur in the vocal department, then as an assistant to Ernst Reichert and finally as head of an opera class. From 1960 to 2007 he was also the artistic director of the Academic Orchestra Association. As a conductor, he has appeared with the Vienna Symphony Orchestra, the Lower Austrian Tonkünstler, the ORF Symphony Orchestra and several Hungarian orchestras, among others. In the 1950s he directed the newly founded Orchestra of Jeunesses Musicales for several years. From 1988 to 2011 Wolfgang Gabriel was artistic director of the Bachgemeinde Wien. Gabriel composed more than 100 works, including several orchestral and instrumental concertos, chamber music (including several string quartets)piano works and numerous song cycles. His tonal language was influened by Béla Bartók, Igor Stravinsky and Alban Berg. He wrote several of his works for his second wife, the singer Susan Dennis, and for his son Martin Gabriel, who is the principal oboist of the Vienna Philharmonic. Source: Wikipedia
